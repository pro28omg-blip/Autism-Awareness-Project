🧠 About Autism

What is Autism?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex developmental condition that involves persistent challenges in social interaction, speech and nonverbal communication, and restricted or repetitive behaviors. The effects of ASD and the severity of symptoms are different in each person.

But more importantly, autism is a different way of experiencing the world. Neurodiversity is the concept that neurological differences are to be recognized and respected as any other human variation. People with autism often have unique strengths, perspectives, and talents.

The infinity symbol (♾) represents the broad and varied spectrum of experiences within the autistic community — celebrating infinite possibilities rather than focusing on a "missing puzzle piece."

★ What We Do

What Is Our Project

The Awareness Program is to challenge these misconceptions and promote inclusion and acceptance because as only a small portion of the population in Pakistan has knowledge about autism.

By fixing this gap in knowledge our project will help reduce misconceptions about Autism and makes the world more informed and supportive for autism awareness.

◆ Aim & Objective

Our Main Aim and Objective

◆

Our objective for creating this committee is to raise awareness for autism and promote inclusion and acceptance.

◆

Our aim is to properly educate Pakistani citizens about autism using accurate studies, i.e., WHO & CDC Studies.

◆

We want to challenge myths and reduce stigma by sharing facts and examples from these studies.

◆

We want to encourage respectful and inclusive behavior in schools, communities and online.

★ Why It Matters

Importance of Research

Studies indicate only 17.6 percent of students and teachers in school know about Autism which indicates how important awareness is because kids or adults with Autism do not know how to properly communicate and interact, Showing signs of Autism is often linked to black magic or evil eye, Parents feel ashamed which should not be the case. We want children suffering with Autism in Pakistan to be recognized and treated the proper way. We want parents to seek psychological attention rather than hiding their children from the community.

▶ Perspectives

National & Global Perspective

National Perspective

Although some efforts are being made to raise awareness by the Government, it clearly isn't enough, as there are 350 000 to 400 000 people diagnosed with Autism in Pakistan. The lack of awareness should be taken account of, However, we can perceive that awareness is slowly being raised but more effort is required in underdeveloped areas of Pakistan

Global Perspective

Many programs are being conducted and researchers worldwide have been taking place in it, these programs are diving deep into the complexity of the minds of children with Autism.

In addition to that, multiple informative sessions are being held, and Teachers are being educated on the signs of Autism, along with how to cope with a child with Autism.

Overall, Awareness is being raised globally, as this problem is faced in many areas other than Pakistan.

★ Key Facts

Fast Facts About Autism

🧠

Autism is a spectrum. Every autistic person has their own distinct set of strengths and challenges — no two people experience it the same way.

👥

About 1 in 36 children has been identified with autism spectrum disorder according to estimates from the CDC's ADDM Network.

💡

Many autistic individuals have exceptional abilities in visual skills, music, academic skills, and rote memory — unique strengths to be celebrated.

❤️

Sensory sensitivities are common. Sounds, lights, textures, or smells might be experienced more intensely by autistic individuals.

How You Can Support

Small actions in our school and community can make a massive difference in creating an inclusive environment.

1

Listen & Learn

Educate yourself about autism. Listen to the voices of autistic individuals when they share their experiences and needs.

2

Be Patient

Give people extra time to process information and respond. Understand that everyone communicates differently and at their own pace.

3

Be Inclusive

Invite autistic peers to participate in activities, but never force them. Create environments where sensory needs are respected.
